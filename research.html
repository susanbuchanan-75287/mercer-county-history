Archives & genealogy

Research & collections.

Our archives house thousands of unique artifacts, documents and genealogical materials. We welcome researchers — a few simple guidelines keep these irreplaceable records safe for the next generation.

Plan a visit

Accessing the archives

By appointment only — please schedule at least one week in advance.

  • MCHS members: free access
  • Non-members: $10/day research fee
  • Book it: email research@mchsprincetonwv.org
Reading-room rules

Protecting the collection

  • Sign the researcher log and present photo ID
  • Only pencils are permitted in the research area
  • No food, drinks, pens or backpacks
  • Personal photography of documents is generally prohibited
Trace your roots

Map your family's Mercer County

Communities move, merge and vanish. Our collection includes historic maps, plat books, cemetery records, church and school registers, newspapers and family files that help you place ancestors in the towns and hollows where they actually lived — from Princeton and Bramwell to the coal camps in between.

Grow the archive

Donating to the collection.

Have a document, photograph or artifact that tells a Mercer County story? We'd love to hear about it.

1 · Start a conversation

Please discuss items with the Curator before drop-off so we can confirm fit and condition.

2 · Deed of Gift

Accepted items require signing a form that transfers legal ownership to the MCHS.

3 · What we collect

We especially seek materials on mining, railroads, agriculture and local organizations.

Please note: The MCHS cannot provide appraisals or valuations of donated items for tax purposes. If you need a valuation, please consult an independent certified appraiser prior to donation.
